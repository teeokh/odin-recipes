Pounded Yam

Description

Pounded yam is a traditional African dish that hails from Nigeria and some other West African countries. It is a popular and well-loved staple food in the region. The dish is made from yam, a starchy tuberous root vegetable that is cooked, pounded, and molded into a smooth, stretchy, and dough-like consistency. Pounded yam is known for its smooth texture and subtle, slightly sweet taste, making it a versatile accompaniment to a wide variety of soups and stews.

Ingredients

  • Yam
  • Water
  • Mortar and Pestle
  1. Peel and Cut the Yam: Start by peeling the yam with a knife to remove the skin. Then, cut the yam into evenly sized chunks. This will help ensure even cooking
  2. Boil the Yam: In a large pot, add enough water to cover the yam chunks. Bring the water to a boil and carefully add the yam pieces. Allow the yam to cook until it becomes soft and tender. You can test the doneness by inserting a fork or knife into the yam; it should easily slide through when it's fully cooked.
  3. Drain the Water: Once the yam is fully cooked, drain the water from the pot, leaving only the cooked yam.
  4. Serve and Enjoy
